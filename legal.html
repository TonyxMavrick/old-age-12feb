Privacy Policy

Information We Collect

ComfortAging collects personal and medical information necessary to provide quality care to our residents. This includes:

  • Personal identification (name, date of birth, contact information)
  • Medical history, current health conditions, and medications
  • Emergency contact information
  • Insurance and billing information
  • Activity and dietary preferences

HIPAA Compliance

We are fully compliant with the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA). All protected health information (PHI) is stored securely and transmitted using industry-standard encryption. Access to medical records is strictly limited to authorized care staff on a need-to-know basis.

How We Use Your Information

Your information is used exclusively for:

  • Providing personalized care and medical treatment
  • Coordinating with healthcare providers and family members
  • Billing and insurance processing
  • Safety monitoring and emergency response
  • Improving our services and resident experience

Family Portal & Communications

Our Family Portal provides secure access for authorized family members to view care updates, activity photos, and schedule information. Access is granted only with explicit resident or legal guardian consent. All portal communications are encrypted and access logs are maintained for security purposes.

Your Rights

You have the right to:

  • Access your medical records at any time
  • Request corrections to your information
  • Receive an accounting of information disclosures
  • Request restrictions on certain uses of your information
  • File a complaint if you believe your privacy rights have been violated

Terms & Conditions

Admission Requirements

Admission to ComfortAging requires a comprehensive health assessment to ensure we can meet the resident's care needs. All residents or their legal representatives must sign a residency agreement outlining care services, fees, and responsibilities.

Payment Terms

Monthly fees are due on the first of each month. We accept private pay, long-term care insurance, and work with Medicare/Medicaid where applicable. Additional services beyond the standard care package may incur extra fees, which will be clearly communicated in advance.

Resident Rights & Responsibilities

All residents have the right to dignity, respect, privacy, and self-determination. This includes the right to participate in care decisions, voice concerns without fear of retaliation, and maintain personal possessions. Residents are expected to treat staff and fellow residents with respect and follow community guidelines.

Termination of Services

Either party may terminate the residency agreement with 30 days written notice. ComfortAging reserves the right to immediate termination in cases of non-payment, aggressive behavior, or when a resident's needs exceed our care capabilities.

Questions About Our Policies?

Our team is happy to discuss any concerns or questions you have about privacy, data protection, or our terms of service.
